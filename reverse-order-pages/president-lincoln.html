2.   Abraham Lincoln

Number two, Abraham Lincoln. Abraham Lincoln, nicknamed 'Honest Abe' was the 16th President of the United States, serving from 1861 until his assassination in 1865. He was born on February 12th, 1809, in Hodgenville, Kentucky. After a brief stint in the Illinois State Legislature, Lincoln became a lawyer and entered politics. He was elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 1846 and served one term. In 1860, Lincoln was elected President of the United States, becoming the first Republican to hold the office. During the Civil War as President, he issued the Emancipation Proclamation, which declared that all slaves in Confederate territory were to be set free. Lincoln was assassinated by John Wilkes Booth on April 14, 1865, while attending a play at Ford's Theatre in Washington, D.C. Like Washington, Lincoln was also commemorated on 2+ tokens of currency with the U.S. Five Dollar Bill and the U.S. Penny Coin.
