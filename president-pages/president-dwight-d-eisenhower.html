4. Dwight D. Eisenhower

Number four, Dwight D. Eisenhower. Dwight D. Eisenhower was the 34th President of the United States, serving from 1953 to 1961. He was born on October 14th, 1890, in Denison, Texas. After graduating from West Point in 1915, Eisenhower served in the U.S. Army during both World War I and World War II. He was appointed as Supreme Commander of the Allied Forces in Europe during World War II and oversaw the D-Day invasion of Normandy. After the war, he served as President of Columbia University before being elected President of the United States in 1952. Eisenhower was the first President to be bound by the 22nd Amendment, which limited the number of terms a President could serve, and he left office in 1961. He died on March 28th, 1969, in Washington D.C. And of course, as with all deceased presidents, Eisenhower was minted on to a U.S. Dollar Coin as apart of the Presidential $1 Coin Program.
